{% trans "Nature-based Solutions (NbS)" %}

{% trans "Nature-based Solutions (NbS) are ‘actions to protect, sustainably manage, and restore natural or modified ecosystems, that address societal challenges, effectively and adaptively, simultaneously providing human well-being and biodiversity benefits’ (IUCN).The concept of NbS arose out of an increasing recognition of the fundamental role ecosystems play in addressing some of society’s biggest challenges, including enhancing water security, reducing risk of natural disasters, avoiding degradation of natural ecosystems, and mitigating or adapting to the impacts of climate change." %}

{% trans "NbS can be categorized by both their intervention type and their habitat. There are four major intervention categories: protection, restoration, management and creation. These intervention types can be carried out across a range of habitats, including forests, grasslands, wetlands, agricultural landscapes and cities" %}

{% trans "Agroforestry activities" %}

{% trans "Agroforestry activities are defined as a series of technologies for land-use in which trees are combined with agricultural crops and / or pastures to increase and optimize production in a sustained manner. The planting of forest crops and trees is used sequentially and in combination, with the application of soil conservation practices. These practices and systems are designed and executed within the context of a farm management plan, where farmer participation is key." %}
